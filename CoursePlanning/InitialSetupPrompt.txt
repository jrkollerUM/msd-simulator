In this folder you will find all of the teaching materials used last semester to teach ME 360 at the University of Michigan. In its current delivery, the course has the following major problems:
-The course is too heavily lecture focused being taught for two hours each session twice a week. We want to redesign the course so that some of the derivation processes and higher level connection of content is offloaded to pre-recorded videos that students watch before each course. These videos should only be ~15-20 minutes each. This offloading some of the content to pre-work allows for more class time to be small group work where students solve through guided problems with those in their group and better apply the content in a structured and supportive setting.
-There is a lot of redundancy in the first few class sessions where we recap a lot of the mathematical foundation for the course and then that same content is seen again in the analysis portion of the course. We think we could solidify this by teaching some of this mathematical foundation while we teach the analysis portion of the course and rely more heavily on the student's prerequisite differential equations knowledge coming into the course. We were thinking of maybe having a first day assessment where students test their knowledge on prerequisite content that they should know from their introduction to dynamics course (ME 240) and differential equations. I teach ME 240 and could include that course content as well if you need that for planning of things. We would then supply a module on Canvas that touches on this prerequisite knowledge for students to review at their own pace as needed given how they did on the first assessment.
-In its current state, there is very little controls taught in the course and we would like to make room for more. Right now we only teach PID control from a fairly high level; however, this is the only required controls theory course in our curriculum and students can take ME 461 if they want to go further. I believe our students should have more controls knowledge coming out of this course and program if they choose not to take that elective. You can use the required text as reference for possible topics to include and cross reference with other university curriculums.
